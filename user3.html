Community Reports

Pranay

State:Maharasthra

River:Panchganga

Description: I am Pranay, and I have always felt a deep connection to the Panchganga River. Growing up near its banks, I remember the serene beauty of the flowing water and the lush greenery that surrounded it. The Panchganga has been an integral part of our culture and livelihood, supporting agriculture and providing water for daily needs. Unfortunately, this precious river is now under threat due to alarming levels of pollution. The sources of pollution are numerous—industrial waste, untreated sewage, and agricultural runoff are contaminating the waters, leading to a significant decline in water quality. It pains me to see the vibrant aquatic life diminishing and the once-crystal-clear waters turning murky. The situation has escalated to the point where many locals are now hesitant to use the river for bathing and washing, fearing the health risks associated with polluted water. The effects of this pollution are not limited to the river alone. The communities surrounding the Panchganga are facing serious health issues due to contaminated water. Skin diseases and other waterborne illnesses have become more prevalent, affecting children and the elderly the most. We cannot afford to remain passive any longer. It is crucial for us, as a community, to come together to combat this crisis. We must advocate for stricter regulations on waste management, organize awareness campaigns about the importance of keeping our rivers clean, and engage in regular clean-up drives. The Panchganga River deserves our respect and protection. Let’s unite to restore its natural beauty and ensure that it remains a source of life for future generations. Together, we can make a difference.
