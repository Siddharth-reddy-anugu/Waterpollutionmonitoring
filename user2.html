Community Reports

Karthika

State:Telangana

River:Musi

Description:I am Karthika, and I’ve always felt a deep sense of belonging to the Musi River, which flows through my city. Growing up near its banks, I’ve witnessed the river’s transformation over the years, from a source of life to a victim of pollution. The Musi has been an integral part of our community, providing water for drinking, irrigation, and recreation. However, it pains me to see how neglect and pollution have tarnished its once-pristine waters. The primary contributors to the pollution of the Musi River are industrial waste, untreated sewage, and solid waste dumping. Factories along the banks discharge hazardous chemicals, while the absence of proper waste management leads to an influx of plastic and other debris. The water, once clear and vibrant, is now a murky reflection of our environmental neglect. The impact of this pollution extends far beyond the river itself. The communities surrounding the Musi are suffering from a range of health issues, including skin diseases and waterborne illnesses. It is heartbreaking to see families affected by the very river that once nourished them. As a concerned citizen, I believe it’s crucial for us to take action. We need to raise awareness about the importance of the Musi River and advocate for strict regulations on waste disposal. Community involvement is key; organizing clean-up drives and promoting eco-friendly practices can help restore the river’s health. The Musi River is a vital part of our heritage and ecosystem. Let’s come together to protect it, ensuring that it remains a source of life and beauty for generations to come. Together, we can make a difference and bring back the Musi River to its former glory.
