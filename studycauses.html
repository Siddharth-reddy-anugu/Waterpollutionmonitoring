Causes of Water Pollution

Water pollution is primarily caused by human activities that introduce harmful substances into water bodies. Below are the major sources of water pollution:

1. Industrial Waste

Industries often discharge pollutants directly into nearby water sources. Chemicals, heavy metals, and toxic waste are some of the harmful substances found in industrial discharge, contaminating water and harming aquatic life.

2. Agricultural Runoff

Fertilizers, pesticides, and animal waste from farms can wash into water bodies during rainfall, causing nutrient pollution. Excessive nutrients lead to algal blooms, which deplete oxygen levels in water and harm aquatic organisms.

3. Plastic Waste

Plastic materials such as bottles, bags, and microplastics enter oceans and rivers, causing severe damage to marine ecosystems. These materials do not decompose easily and pose a threat to marine animals and fish that ingest them.

4. Sewage and Wastewater

Untreated or poorly treated sewage often finds its way into rivers and lakes, introducing bacteria, viruses, and chemicals that contaminate water supplies. This leads to diseases and environmental degradation.
